Emotional Intelligence

“Emotional intelligence [mass noun] The capacity to be aware of, control, and express one’s emotions, and to handle interpersonal relationships judiciously and empathetically.” Oxford's Lexico

What is Emotional Intelligence?

As the definition outlines, Emotional Intelligence, or EQ, is the ability to recognise and be mindful of the emotions of myself, as well as the emotions of others. The ability to discern and label different feelings as they come, and also being able to adjust themselves according to those emotions is also included as a facet of EQ.

Like all things that are unfamiliar, your Emotional Intelligence grows the more you practice different EQ-related skills. These skills are also known as ‘steps’. These three steps are:

  1. Attention Training
  2. Self knowledge and Mastery
  3. Creating Useful Mental Habits

  1. Attention Training
    Attention Training is basically being able to pay attention to what is happening in our bodies when we feel different emotions. When we first start to mentally be aware of any change in our emotions, our body would have already reacted to the change.
    We can train our attention to notice those changes, and be more aware of the connection between our mind, body and emotions. Doing this helps our minds be more calm, clear and alert.

  2. Self-knowledge and Self Mastery
    Following on from attention training, having a clear and alert mind helps us gain more insight to what is happening inside ourselves, which is what self-knowledge is.
    From having that knowledge of what we’re feeling and how we’re reacting and engaging when those changes happen, we can practice mastery over those reactions when they occur. For example, mentally saying to ourselves, “Okay, so I know what happens when I feel anxious, I know how much anxiety I feel when this thing happens, and I know how to bring myself back.”

  3. Creating Useful Mental Habits
    Whenever those voices of self-doubt, and criticism pop-up during a challenge, it can really throw you off-guard. _Especially_ when you know that you are capable, and that you have the knowledge and tools to help you. This is the same when you start to compare yourself against others and the “superego” starts to come through

How is it different to Intellectual Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is different from Intellectual Intelligence in the way that EQ focuses on how well I understand my own emotions, whereas IQ focuses on my capacity of intellectual intelligence.

Why is it important?

The importance of being Emotionally Intelligent, (or rather, striving to be), ensures that I am able to recognize the emotions I have and adjust my own behaviours in accordance to those emotions in order to relieve stress, overcome challenges while studying, and also be more empathetic with the feelings of those around me. Having a sound understanding of the importance of EQ helps foster skills like effective communication, conflict resolution, and more social awareness.
