My Bootcamp Learning Plan

What are my long term goals and career path?

My goals and career path have basically stayed now that I prepare for Bootcamp. I still want to gain the skills of a full-stack developer, and also in the future, get into Test-Driven Development.

What do you think my biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

After going through my previous strengths and limitations, I think that one of my strengths will be to plan a strategy in order to be able to write the actually code. I also think that my strength in individualisation will be of use in working with the other people who are in my cohort.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

My biggest challenge will be to try and keep to timebox suggestions as well as being able to keep a level head and go for when I will be frustrated.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

The skills I would most like to see developed in myself while at EDA would include better communication skills, more developed sense of the growth mindset, and self-affirmation, as well as a honed-down problem-solving mindset.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Basically, what I expect from the facilitation team would be transparency in communication, regular catch-ups, and support and advice.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

One of the conditions I had placed on myself before even applying for the programme is that I would seek and ask for help, as much as I am able, whenever I would be stuck. I fully intend to be committed to seeking help through EDA’s Slack foundations and code-help-desk channels, search for a solution in the channels before asking my own question and also try to keep in regular contact with other learners and facilitators.

My Foundations Learning Plan

My long term goals and ideal career pathway

I chose to join and grow with EDA in order to gain the skills of a full-stack developer and be able to work for myself, collaborate with designers and pursue personal projects. Eventually, however, I do want to apply these skills into the stream of software testing, (and still develop on the side).

Non-technical skills would I like to see developed in myself while at EDA

The skills I would most like to see developed in myself while at EDA would include better communication skills, more developed sense of the growth mindset, and self-affirmation, as well as a honed-down problem-solving mindset.

My strengths and limitations? (How would I apply these strengths, effectively & productively, to manage my workload, and while working with other learners, despite my limitations)

I think that out of the strengths from that list, the ones that will help me the most with my learning Ideation, Individualization, and Strategy. I actually feel that all five could cross over easily, and be of benefit to me in my journey. Where I lack in focus, calmness, timeboxing, I could use strategies to help combat and overcome those obstacles. The Problem Solving Process and Framework would be beneficial as a strategic guide. Individualization would be really helpful when the time comes to pair with another cohort buddy, as I love getting to know the people I work with in a way that allows for a more harmonious collaboration.

Based on these strengths, and limitations, I am fully aware of the possibilities that I could be completely overwhelmed and quit before I start. As mentioned in my previous description, applying the strategies of The Problem Solving Process and Framework, as well as keeping a regular check on the timeboxing guidelines when I feel myself feeling overwhelmed, would be useful while I try to manage my workload during this programme. I will also make sure to be able to communicate my intentions with other learners, facilitators and representatives, in order to work productively and safely.

A commitment to seeking help when I need it

One of the conditions I had placed on myself before even applying for the programme is that I would seek and ask for help, as much as I am able, whenever I would be stuck. I fully intend to be committed to seeking help through EDA’s Slack foundations and code-help-desk channels, search for a solution in the channels before asking my own question and also try to keep in regular contact with other learners and facilitators.

My expectations from the facilitation team

Basically, what I expect from the facilitation team would be transparency in communication, regular catch-ups, and support and advice.

My block-out times

The block-out times for me _until bootcamp_ would be two-three hours on Monday nights, as I spend time with my family when everyone is home, and occasionally two hours in the evening on Thursdays and Tuesdays. I also have a fortnightly 1hr-therapy session every second week, which is aimed to take place around 2.30~3pm during a weekday.
