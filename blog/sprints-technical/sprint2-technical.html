Technical Blog: Margins, Borders, Padding?

What is the difference between Margin, Border, or Padding?



Margins, Borders and Padding are important when it comes to laying out a website. It gives each item that you see on this page space around each other and makes the content on a website more readable, (and in my opinion, more attractive - I definitely do not like a cramped webpage!) Let's breakdown what Margin, Border and Padding mean:


  • Margin: Margins are the space/s between elements
  • Padding: Padding is the space inside an element
  • Border: Borders are basically frames around elements. They are commonly seen as solid, dotted or dashed


(if you didn't notice, I used a double border around that list!)


The uses for margins, padding and borders come in handy when we want to create space between different elements, section elements away from others or make sure that what you see on screen is in line with each other.
